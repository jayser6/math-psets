\section{Bilinear Forms}
\subsection*{1.1} 
\newcommand{\symform}[2]{\form{#1}{#2}_{sym}}
\newcommand{\skewform}[2]{\form{#1}{#2}_{sk}}
Suppose such a decomposition exists: 
given $\form{}{}$, let $\symform{}{}$ and $\skewform{}{}$ be symmetric and skew-symmetric forms on $V$ such that for all $v, w \in V$, 
\begin{equation} \label{eq:8_1_1_formdef} 
  \form{v}{w} = \symform{v}{w} + \skewform{v}{w}.
\end{equation}
Then by \eqref{eq:8_1_1_formdef}, 
\begin{align*} 
  \form{v}{w} + \form{w}{v} & = 2\symform{v}{w} \\ 
  \form{v}{w} - \form{w}{v} & = 2\skewform{v}{w}
\end{align*}
So one can just define the forms $\symform{v}{w} \defeq \frac{1}{2}(\form{v}{w} + \form{w}{w})$ and $\skewform{v}{w} \defeq \frac{1}{2}(\form{v}{w} - \form{v}{w})$ and check that 
\begin{enumerate}
  \item $\symform{}{}$ and $\skewform{}{}$ are indeed bilinear forms 
  \item $\symform{}{}$ is symmetric, while $\skewform{}{}$ is skew-symmetric
  \item For all $v, w \in V$, $\form{v}{w} = \symform{v}{w} + \skewform{v}{w}$. 
\end{enumerate}
